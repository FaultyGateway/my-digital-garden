If you plug in a Dell charger and the ring light goes off, there is likely some power delivery fault in the laptop itself, not the charger.
You can double check the chargers amps and try another charger but failing that, the laptop will get sent to be recycled/sold bulk.

>[!tip] When making changes to the BIOS, you can save time by hitting Apply, waiting till it greys out, and then hitting the power button on the laptop. After a second it will turn off completely and you dont have to wait on it to restart or hold down the power button to force it off. This can save time if you are just evaluating a laptop before putting a drive in it to proceed with final testing.

If a Dell doesn't turn on, it could be the power jack, charger, or power regulation chip.

- Power jacks are generally swappable between models and are attached w/ a cable you can disconnect from the motherboard. Swap in a different jack and see if it charges/recognizes the charger. You can check in the BIOS for a live readout of the battery and charger status.

- Charger could be bad. Does it work w/ another known good laptop? Does the charger light come on, but then turn off when plugged into a laptop? It could be the laptop has a short and the charger is turning itself off as a safety mechanism.

- Nothing worked? Could be the power regulation chip. Unplug charger, battery and cmos. Hold down power button for 60-80 seconds to fully deplete the capacitors. Replug the battery and cmos, plug in charger and check for power/charge light.
